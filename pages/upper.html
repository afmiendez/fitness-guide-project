Upper day

During a upper day, the muscles targeted are the chest, back, triceps and biceps. All exercises were already performed on previous days, with this upper workout, it becomes possible to train the same muscles twice a week. If possible try to complete the exercises in their respective order. A table with all exercises and their respective reps and sets will be available at the bottom of the page

Bench Press (8x3)

The bench press is a great way to build the chest and triceps. It is also very effective at stretching the muscle fibers at the bottom of the repetition which is crucial for muscle hypertrophy. Aim to complete 8 reps and 3 sets and should try to come close to failure during your last 2 reps on the last 2 sets. Rest between 3-4 minutes every set. Watch the following video for a demonstration.

Incline Smith Bench Press (8x3)

The incline smith bench press is also a great way to build the chest and triceps, with special focus on the upper chest. It is also very effective at stretching the muscle fibers at the bottom of the repetition which is crucial for muscle hypertrophy. Aim to complete 8 reps and 3 sets and try to come close to failure during your last 2 reps on the last 2 sets. Rest between 3-4 minutes every set. Watch the following video for a demonstration.

Peck Deck Fly (8x3)

The peck deck fly is the perfect chest exercise for a full stretch, control the eccentric while doing the repetitions. Aim to complete 8 reps and 3 sets and try to come close to failure during your last 2 reps on the last 2 sets. Rest between 3-4 minutes every set. Watch the following video for a demonstration.

Lat Pulldown (8x3)

The lat pulldown is a great way to build the upper and lower lats as well as the biceps. It is also very effective at providing a full range of motion during the repetition which is recommended for muscle hypertrophy. Aim to complete 8 reps and 3 sets and should try to come close to failure during your last 2 reps on the last 2 sets. Rest between 3-4 minutes every set. Watch the following video for a demonstration.

Barbell Back Row (8x3)

The dumbbell back row is a great way to build the upper and lower lats as well as the rhomboids. It is also very effective at providing a full range of motion during the repetition which is recommended for muscle hypertrophy. Aim to complete 8 reps and 3 sets and should try to come close to failure during your last 2 reps on the last 2 sets. Rest between 3-4 minutes every set. Watch the following video for a demonstration.

Lat Pullover (8x3)

The lat pullover is a great way to build the upper and lower lats, focus on stretching at the top and contracting at the bottom. It is also very effective at providing a full range of motion during the repetition which is recommended for muscle hypertrophy. Aim to complete 8 reps and 3 sets and should try to come close to failure during your last 2 reps on the last 2 sets. Rest between 3-4 minutes every set. Watch the following video for a demonstration.

Tricep Extensions (8x3)

Now for the triceps section, the first exercise is the tricep extensions which is great to target the lateral head of the tricep. Aim to complete 8 reps and 3 sets and try to come close to failure during your last 2 reps on the last 2 sets. Rest between 3-4 minutes every set. Watch the following video for a demonstration.

Overhead Tricep Extensions (8x3)

For the final exercise, overhead tricep extensions are amazing to target the long head of the tricep. Aim to complete 8 reps and 3 sets and try to come close to failure during your last 2 reps on the last 2 sets. Rest between 3-4 minutes every set. Watch the following video for a demonstration.

Preacher Bicep Curl (8-10x3)

The preacher bicep curl is a great way to build the biceps, focus on contracting at the top. Aim to complete 8-10 reps and 3 sets and try to come close to failure during your last 2 reps on the last 2 sets. Rest between 3-4 minutes every set. Watch the following video for a demonstration.

Rope Hammer Curl (8-10x3)

The rope hammer curl is a great way to build the biceps and the brachialis, focus on contracting at the top. Aim to complete 8-10 reps and 3 sets and try to come close to failure during your last 2 reps on the last 2 sets. Rest between 3-4 minutes every set. Watch the following video for a demonstration.

Tips

  • Always warmup before starting your workouts. It avoids injuries and gets you ready to push yourself even further.
  • If you feel your forearms giving up before your back on a pull day, you can always buy straps to help holding weights.
  • Take your time adjusting to heavy weights, it is not worth risking an injury to try and impress others.

Nutrition

  • Don't forget about hydration throughout the day, with special focus during the workouts
  • Make sure to ingest a diet high in protein, with the recommended amount of daily protein intake (grams) being double your bodyweight in kilograms.
  • Calculate your maintenance calories to see how much do you need to eat daily to gain weight or to lose it.
